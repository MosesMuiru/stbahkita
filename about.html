About us

St. Bakhita vocational training center enrolls disadvantaged girls and women to learn hairdressing and dressmaking. They undergo a 12-month program, preparing for grade 3 NITA Trade Examination in December. Additionally, they gain practical experience through a 3-month attachment. Qualified teachers ensure their skills meet market demands. Students produce school uniforms and fulfill contracts for neighboring schools.

Empowering Careers

Recognizing the importance of career development, we have partnered with ELDOHUB, an I.C.T. and entrepreneurship innovation hub. They offer a one-month intensive course in entrepreneurship and career development, providing young individuals with practical skills, networking opportunities, and guidance in building solid business plans for their respective career paths.

Entrepreneurial Edge

To address the need for comprehensive empowerment, we partnered with ELDOHUB, an I.C.T. and entrepreneurship innovation hub. They offer a one-month intensive course in entrepreneurship and career development, providing workspace and networking opportunities for young, creative individuals. The program focuses on practical application and creating a solid business plan tailored to individual ventures.

Assessment and certification

The Technical and Vocational Education and Training Authority (TVETA) conducts assessments and certifications for our students in dressmaking and hairdressing through the National Industrial Training Authority (NITA) examination in December. Certification is granted to those who meet the competency standards and achieve grade 3 in the NITA examination.
